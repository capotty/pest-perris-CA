Common Signs You Need Squirrel Removal Services

Being vigilant and recognizing the signs of a squirrel infestation early on can save you time, money, and stress. Here are some telltale indicators that squirrels have taken up residence in your Perris home:

Scratching and Scampering Sounds

One of the most common indicators of a squirrel infestation is the sound of scratching, scampering, or gnawing coming from your attic, walls, or ceiling. Squirrels are most active during the early morning and late afternoon, so you're more likely to hear these noises around those times. If you notice these sounds, it's a sign that squirrels are exploring and nesting within your home's structure, necessitating prompt

Visible Squirrel Activity

Seeing squirrels frequently entering or exiting your home is a clear sign that they have established a nest inside. Pay attention to areas where squirrels seem to congregate, such as near your roof, vents, or chimney. If you spot squirrels repeatedly going in and out of these areas, it's a strong indication that they have found a way inside and are using your home as their personal haven.

Damaged Entry Points

Squirrels are notorious for chewing through various materials to gain entry into buildings. Inspect your roof, soffits, fascia, and vents for signs of damage, such as gnaw marks, holes, or tears. Squirrels can also damage weather stripping around doors and windows, creating entry points for themselves and other pests. Addressing these damaged entry points promptly is crucial to prevent squirrels from accessing your home and causing further destruction.

Nesting Materials

Squirrels build nests using a variety of materials, including leaves, twigs, paper, and insulation. If you find these materials scattered around your property, particularly near potential entry points, it's a sign that squirrels may be nesting nearby. In some cases, you may even find nests inside your attic, chimney, or other secluded areas. Removing nesting materials and sealing off entry points can help discourage squirrels from taking up residence in your home.

Droppings and Urine Stains

Squirrel droppings and urine can contaminate your home and create unpleasant odors. Look for droppings in your attic, basement, or other areas where squirrels may be active. Urine stains may also be visible on ceilings or walls. Squirrel waste can pose health risks, so it's important to clean and sanitize affected areas thoroughly. Contacting professionals for squirrel removal in Lake Elsinore will ensure that droppings are properly taken care of.

Chewed Wires

Squirrels have a habit of gnawing on electrical wires, which can create a fire hazard. If you notice frayed or chewed wires in your home, it's a sign that squirrels may be present. Chewing wires can also cause power outages and damage to electrical appliances. Protecting your wires from being susceptible to rodents is of the utmost importance for the safety of your property. It's essential to address this issue promptly to prevent electrical fires and ensure the safety of your family.

Increased Energy Bills

If you notice a sudden increase in your energy bills, it could be due to squirrels damaging your insulation. Squirrels often tear apart insulation to create nests, which can reduce its effectiveness. Damaged insulation forces your heating and cooling systems to work harder to maintain a comfortable temperature, resulting in higher energy consumption. Repairing or replacing damaged insulation can help restore your home's energy efficiency and lower your utility bills. Contacting us at Perris Pest Control Company will mean you are one step closer to lowering your energy bills!

Foul Odors

Squirrels can leave behind unpleasant odors, especially if they die inside your home. The smell of decaying squirrels can be quite strong and can attract other pests, such as flies and rodents. If you notice a foul odor coming from your attic, walls, or other areas of your home, it's important to investigate and remove any dead squirrels promptly. Professional pest control services can help locate and remove dead animals, as well as sanitize the affected areas to eliminate odors.

The Dangers of Ignoring a Squirrel Infestation

Ignoring a squirrel infestation can lead to a multitude of problems, ranging from property damage to health hazards. Understanding the potential risks can motivate prompt action to safeguard your home and family.

Property Damage

Squirrels are notorious for causing extensive damage to homes. They can chew through electrical wires, leading to potential fire hazards. Additionally, they may damage insulation, causing it to lose its effectiveness and resulting in higher energy bills. Squirrels also tend to gnaw on wooden structures, such as beams, siding, and roofing, compromising the structural integrity of your home. The longer a squirrel infestation persists, the more extensive the damage becomes, leading to costly repairs. You also want to ensure that wires like these do not become exposed. Keeping your wires protected will benefit you in the short and long term.

Health Risks

Squirrels can carry diseases and parasites that pose health risks to humans and pets. They may harbor fleas, ticks, and mites, which can transmit diseases such as Lyme disease and Rocky Mountain spotted fever. Squirrel droppings and urine can also contaminate surfaces and create unsanitary conditions. Exposure to squirrel waste can lead to respiratory problems, allergic reactions, and other health issues. Prompt removal of squirrels and thorough sanitization of affected areas are crucial to minimize health risks.

Fire Hazards

Squirrels have a habit of gnawing on electrical wires, which can create a significant fire hazard. Damaged wires can short circuit, causing sparks and potentially igniting nearby flammable materials. In addition to chewing wires, squirrels may also build nests near electrical components, further increasing the risk of fire. Addressing squirrel-related electrical damage promptly is essential to prevent fires and ensure the safety of your home and occupants.

Contamination

Squirrel droppings and urine can contaminate insulation, ductwork, and other areas of your home. This contamination can create unpleasant odors and attract other pests, such as insects and rodents. Additionally, squirrel waste can harbor bacteria and fungi, which can lead to health problems if inhaled or ingested. Proper cleaning and sanitization of affected areas are necessary to eliminate contamination and create a healthy living environment. We at Perris Pest Control are committed to providing this healthy living environment.

Attracting Other Pests

A squirrel infestation can attract other pests to your home. Squirrel nests and droppings can provide food and shelter for insects such as cockroaches, ants, and flies. Additionally, the presence of squirrels may attract larger pests, such as rats and mice, which are drawn to the same food sources and shelter. Addressing a squirrel infestation promptly can help prevent a domino effect of pest problems in your home. If you think you may have a rodent problem check out our rodent control services!

Structural Damage

Over time, persistent squirrel activity can cause significant structural damage to your home. Squirrels may chew through wooden beams, support columns, and other structural components, compromising the integrity of your home's framework. This damage can weaken your home's ability to withstand weather events and can create safety hazards for occupants. Addressing squirrel-related structural damage promptly is crucial to prevent further deterioration and ensure the safety and stability of your home.

Reduced Property Value

A squirrel infestation can negatively impact the value of your home. Potential buyers may be deterred by the presence of squirrels and the potential for property damage. Even if the infestation is resolved before selling your home, the lingering signs of squirrel activity, such as damaged insulation or chewed wires, can raise concerns among buyers. Addressing a squirrel infestation promptly and thoroughly can help preserve the value of your home and make it more appealing to potential buyers.
